Generation 9 Raid Guide

Six Star Pokémon (G - H)

Gallade (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Psycho Cut:Category: Physical / Power: 70 / Accuracy: 100%
Has an increased critical hit ratio
Close Combat:Category: Physical / Power: 120 / Accuracy: 100%
Lowers the users Defense and Special Defense stat by one stage each
Will-O-Wisp:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: 85%
Inflicts a burn on the target, does not effect Fire types
Aerial Ace:Category: Physical / Power: 60 / Accuracy: - %
Bypasses accuracy checks to always hit
Hypnosis:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: 60%
Puts the target to sleep
Disable:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: 100%
Disables the last move a target used for 4 turns, will fail if the target is already Disabled
Psychic Terrain:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
  • - Boosts the power of Psychic-type moves used by affected Pokémon by 30%
  • - Affected Pokémon become immune to opponents' moves with increased priority
Abilities:
Steadfast:Category: Status
Increases the Pokémons Speed stat by one stage whenever it flinches
Sharpness:Category: Status
Boosts the strength of slicing moves used by 50%
Justified:Category: Status
When hit by a Dark move its Attack is increased by one stage
Tips:
Combination Psycho Cut, Psychic Terrain, and Sharpness will Base Damage 136.5 STAB damage with a higher chance to Critical Hit. On top of that, it can Disable super effective moves and put your Pokémon to sleep requiring a heal cheer to wake up.
Absolutely consider bringing a Pokémon with Taunt to keep your team safe from Gallades status moves. Otherwise Reflect will be super helpful softening Gallades Physical attacks. And if you don't have Taunt, consider removing Psychic Terrain with moves like Electric Terrain (which will also negate effects from Hypnosis), Grassy Terrain, Misty Terrain, Ice Spinner, Steel Roller and Defog.
Gallade has Low Health and Low Defense, so bring a Physical attacker with a High Defense, and avoid Dark moves to make this an easy fight.

Garchomp (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:

Outrage:Category: Physical / Power: 120 / Accuracy: 100%
The user is locked into this move for 2-3 turns, after all turns are finished, or it gets interupted, it will become confused
Earthquake:Category: Physical / Power: 100 / Accuracy: 100%
If the target is using Dig when this move goes into effect, the target will take double damage
Flamethrower:Category: Special / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 100%
Has a 10% chance to cause burn
Rock Slide:Category: Physical/ Power: 75 / Accuracy: 90%
Has a 30% chance to cause flinching
Swords Dance:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Increases the users Attack stat by two stages
Abilities:
Sand Veil:Category: Status
During a sandstorm this Pokémons evasiveness is increased; it also will not take damage from sandstorm
Rough Skin:Category: Status
When hit by a move that makes contact, the attacking Pokémon takes damage equal to 1/8th of its Maximum Health
Tips:
Garchomp is another psuedo-legendary with High Attack. It has good move coverage and Swords Dance gives Outrage and Earthquake a larger damage potential on top of STAB damage.
Bring a Pokémon with Taunt to remove the Attack boost from Swords Dance and Reflect to weaken moves like Outrage and Earthquake. Consider also bringing a Pokémon with the ability Levitate to completely bypass Earthquake, or a Fairy Type to bypass Outrage.
The Defense and Special Defense of this Pokémon are pretty close, however if you have to contend with Rough Skin, you're absolutely going to want to bring a Special Attacker with High Defense.

  • - Prevents affected Pokémon from being afflicted by status conditions

  • - Affected Pokémon become immune to opponents' moves with increased priority

    Gardevoir only has 2 possible damaging moves, but has the ability to boost their damage with the help of its other 3 status moves.
  • - Moonblast with Misty Terrain will hit for 142.5 STAB damage
  • - Psychic with Psychic Terrain will hit for 135 STAB damage
  • - A single Calm Mind will raise these two attacks potential damage by 1 stage
Pokemon with Taunt are mandatory here. Not only will it prevent Terrain changes from Misty Terrain and Psychic Terrain, but it will also prevent Calm Mind and Thunder Wave. If you don't have Taunt you can use combinations with Electric Terrain, Grassy Terrain, Ice Spinner, Steel Roller, Defog, Light Screen, and Safeguard.
You can overwhelm Gardeviors Low Health and Defense with a strong Physical Attacker (which will remove fear of secondary effects from Moonblast) and a strong Special Defense (to guard against a Strong Special Attack and secondary effects from Psychic).

Garganacl (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Stone Edge:Category: Physical / Power: 100 / Accuracy: 80%
Has an increased critical hit ratio
Heavy Slam:Category: Physical / Power: - / Accuracy: 100%
The lighter the target is in comparison to the user, the greater the power (Garganacls weight is 529 lbs. or 240 kg. for this comparrison)
Salt Cure:Category: Physical / Power: 40 / Accuracy: 100%
Inflicts 1/8th of the targets maximum HP as damage per turn in addition to the damage dealt when it is used, this number doubles to 1/4th if the target is Steel or Water type
Hammer Arm:Category: Physical / Power: 100 / Accuracy: 90%
Lowers the users Speed by one stage
Rock Slide:Category: Physical / Power: 75 / Accuracy: 90%
Has a 30% chance of causing each target to flinch
Sandstorm:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
  • - This move will last for 5 turns, while in effect, all Pokémon who are neither Steel, Rock, nor Ground type will be damaged 1/16th of their maximum HP at the end of each turn
  • - All Rock Pokémon have their Special Defense raised by 50% for the duration of the sandstorm
Abilities:
Purifying Salt:Category: Status
Grants immunity from all status conditions and halves the Attack and Special Attack during damage calculation if targeted by a Ghost move
Sturdy:Category: Status
Does not effect Raid
Clear Body:Category: Status
Prevents stat reduction caused by other Pokémons moves and Abilities
Tips:
    Garganacl is the type of Pokémon that will wear you down if you can't get past its defenses
  • - Combination Sandstorm and Salt Cure are going to do 1/16th and 1/8th maximum health damage each turn
  • - Its Defense is its highest base stat followed by its Health/Attack and Special Defense
The best defense against this Pokémon is to bring another Rock or Ground type with High Defense. If your Rock or Ground type is a high weight class, you're going to see added benefits from a low damage Heavy Slam. The temptation to bring a Steel type for Sandstorm immunity and Water type for reduced Rock damage will bite back with double effective damage from Salt Cure, which means you're losing 25% of your health every turn as passive damage. And Ghost moves up against a Garganacl with Purifying Salt will be equally frustrating.
Also unfortunately, terrain moves or weather moves will not effect Garganacl or its moveset. The only real benefit from status moves you're going to see here is Taunt to block Sandstorm and Reflect to lessen the damage from Garganacls Physical Attacks. However because Garganacl is such a tough defense to break, I would recommend avoiding support class here all together as bringing another Attacker will help tremendously.
The good news is that you can bring whatever attacker you prefer as long as you're ready to buff your defensive stats to handle a beating. And because Garganacls moves Salt Cure and Sandstorm take two turns to set up, it means you're getting two free hits on it before facing some real damage. Don't draw the fight out and you can win this.

Cursed Body cannot be avoided by using Protective Pads or a Punching Glove.

    The main focus you should have in a fight against Glaceon are its Tera Type, and its ability to use stat altering and status moves to deal huge damage.
  • - Tera Blast uses the type advantages of its Tera Type to deal special effective damage, so you need to worry about more type disadvantages than Grass, Ground, Dragon, or Flaying
  • - Snowscape will boost its defense by 50% and guarantee a 110 Base STAB from Blizzard
  • - Calm Mind will boost a moveset that tears through your health
Pokémon with Taunt are great here to prevent Snowscape, Calm mind, and Charm but even just having Sunny Day, or Rain Dance will help a lot.
Glaceon isn't going to be as easy to deal with as other Eeveelutions. Yes it has low Health, but it has a pretty formidable Defense that makes going with a Special Attacker here more of an advantage, especially if you dont have Taunt to deal with Charm. In terms of Defense, you're only going to need a Strong Special Defense as Glaceon cannot do Physical Damage.

  • - Don't be surprised if a Gyarados uses Dragon Dance multiple times in a row before attacking

  • - Between Intimidate and secondary effects from Crunch you will face double Defensive stat drops
There's not a lot you can do to prevent Gyarados from very quickly building itself to one shot your whole team. Bring a Pokémon with High Speed and use Taunt immediately. Sunny Day will also help reduce damage from Aqua Tail, and Reflect will do more to reduce damage from all of its moves.
Abilities like Dry Skin and Storm Drain will help with Aqua Tail as well, but note that Sunny Day will damage Pokémon with Dry Skin.
As funny as it sounds Klefki is a really good matchup for Gyarados, it's immune to Dragon moves, and resistant to Ice, Flying, and Dark. Consider Prankster Ability with moveset consisting of: Foul Play, Reflect, Misty Terrain, and Play Rough. Another good matchup is Tinkaton with Mold Breaker, Foul Play, Reflect, Swords Dance, and Gigaton Hammer.
Gyarados has lower Defense compared to its other stats so you'll want to bring a Physical Attacker with High Defense. Focus on staying alive to reduce boosts from Moxie and work with your Team to prevent Gyarados from using Dragon Dance. Note that Gyarados is 4X weak to Electric moves especially useful if it also causes Paralysis.
